About me

I'm Anne. The Architect of Abstraction.

I am a System Architect and Software Researcher specializing in the intersection of computational logic and complex signal environments. My work is defined by a 30-year expertise in high-level signal processing and integration, ranging from the structural analysis of ancient acoustic instruments to the development of cutting-edge, AI-integrated hardware.

I treat software architecture with the same rigor as a master-level orchestral score: emphasizing clean code, modularity, and harmonic system flow. As a pioneer in technical production, I have engineered and recorded over 10 albums, bridging the gap between ancient acoustic preservation and modern digital synthesis.

Currently, I am applying this history of master-level systems thinking to build resilient software solutions, NLP models, and predictive analytics at Columbia University, building on a foundation of doctoral research in Acoustic-Digital integration.
